I am an IT researcher and software engineer currently seeking for a job to contribute and change the world.

My research focuses on software dependability, i.e., the ability of the system to avoid service failures, and on the struggle to assess it and improve it in modern software systems.
I am engaged in providing concrete and easy-to-use methodologies and tools that could help industries and open-source communities to provide software that can be trusted. The challenges arise from the nature of modern systems, complex and fragmented, that makes actual acceptance criteria for non-functional requirements incomplete or hard to apply in practice.
My research interests widen to software security and distributed systems, but I am also looking forward to the Internet of Things (IoT) and Artificial Inteligence (AI).

I got my PhD in Information Technologies and Electrical Engineering, at the University of Naples Federico II.
My PhD tutors were prof. Domenico Cotroneo, and Dr. Roberto Natella. During my PhD course, I was a visiting scholar at Northeastern University (Boston, US) for the academic year 2016/2017, tutored by prof. Cristina Nita-Rotaru.
Prior to joining the PhD course, I obtained my B.Sc. (October 2011) and M.Sc. (January 2014) in Computer Science Engineering, both summa cum laude, from University of Naples Federico II.

I'm a contributor to the FЯIDA project: more precisely, to the low-level code instrumentation library frida-gum.

I'm currently working as a research fellow at DIETI, University of Naples Federico II, on Android Fuzz Testing and Bug Pattern Analysis from public repositories through Machine Learning techniques.
